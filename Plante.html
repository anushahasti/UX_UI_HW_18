Plantae App

​ Plantae aims to guide curious plant owners and enthusiasts with plantcare tips and guidelines. It provides resources to people of all expertise levels.

Role :

UX Designer

Tools :

Figma, Invision, Miro, Flowmap, Zoom, Trello

Team :

Isabella Santini, Jasmin Alvarado



The Challenge

​ Our team is passionate about growing plants, and loving greenery naturally, so we Instantly connected to a point on taking care of plants and various difficulties in finding appropriate plants according to climate and lifestyle. These discussions on plants made us question ourselves on how can we provide a solution for plant owners and also encourage and attract newbies towards growing plants using our Platae mobile app

The Approach

​ We started getting into user thoughts through interviews and surveys. After gathering and collecting all the pain points, we started to imagine users through our Proto-persona. This helped us brainstorm and figure out the features that users would wish to see in Plantae. Further, we were able to successfully design and prototype features with some iterations through user testings and feedback. Our goal was to give plant owners and enthusiasts, a more personable, interactive, and innovative experience through our PLANTAE app


The Process

Research

​All those who love plants, but do not know much about them, or people who want to stay organized with their plant care. We assume they are our participants and through them, we will dive into learning about the challenges that plant owners face. We want to understand how they feel about their current plant care routine and what would help them take better care of their plant buddies.

User Research plan

  • Determine research objective.
  • Determine top research questions
  • Create and utilize an interview script that will lead to the answers our of top research objectives + questions.
  • Interview a sample of 5-6 subjects who enjoy growing plants using the interview script.
  • Transcribe interviews (via google transcribe) for qualitative data.
  • Send out a survey with questions for additional quantitative data.

Research Objectives

  • As user researchers, we want to uncover the top priorities of plant lovers.
  • As user researchers, discover common deterrents for users when caring for a plant
  • As user researchers, we want to know their existing knowledge of plants and plant care.

What do we want to learn about our users?



What are the users' top priorities regarding their plant interests?

What do users enjoy the most about plants and plant care?

How much knowledge do users already have on their plant interests?

How much do users research prior to purchasing or caring for a plant?

What are the top deterrents for users when deciding to maintain a plant?

Key Findings & Insights

Survey Data

Here is the data that was collected from a total of 40 survey responses.

Affinity Diagram

We gathered all of our data from 6 persons into an affinity diagram to answer the question: "what are the most common points we see here?". From the data collected, we were able to narrow down our data to into few groups such as plant preferences, care, deterrents and existing knowledge.

User Persona

We were overwhelmed with the survey responses and the amount of detail our users provided in our interviews. We were able to create a frame work for our user persona. Juhi here represents the anticipated overall Plantae users.

juhi - we see our user persona as someone who is established or beginning to be, and looking to start focusing her energy in adding greenery to her space, but needed guidance to do this successfully. Throughout the rest of the design process we made sure to reference Juhi as a reminder of who we were designing for through furthuer design process , flows of testing and iterations.

Problem Statement

Users who are ready to add new responsibilities to their routine or developing an interest in plants. But they find it difficult to maintain the plant health, determine the appropriate plants for their lifestyles and easily access botanical care resources.

Competitor Analysis

Something that was quite surprising to us was the number of apps that existed with similar concepts to ours. So we realized our biggest threat was COMPETITION and we wanted to make sure our app was unique and provided additional value to the user. We considered Vera, Planta and Plant lens as three direct competitors to our app

What do we want to learn about our users?

Strengths in other apps

  • Scanner for identification and diagnosis.
  • Easy to maneuver through applications (user flow)
  • All apps appear minimal and easy to view on screen

Weaknesses in other app also to make sure not to repeat

  • Overall, the icons are not consistent with the content
  • Home pages are not developed, no call to action
  • Lack of content organization on landing pages

Threats to Plantae

  • ​Planta app has everything a plant owner needs, Plant Lens offers unique features, and Vera appeals to a specific audience based on aesthetic​

Opportunities to improve

  • Some functions could be organized differently. (hierarchy)
  • Can develop more on home or landing pages since many are missing call to actions

Testing

Usability test for low-fidelity prototype

After creating our wireframes and first iteration of a user flow, we went straight into testing of our main features of the app. It was important for us to iron out any issues prior to building our high-fidelity prototypes. We wanted to ensure our designs were easy to understand, with familiar flows and concepts.

Objective : To confirm if users can successfully complete the given task.

  • Task 1: Create Account

    Success Criteria: User reaches the Home Page screen

  • Task 2: Add a plant to your plant profile

    Success Criteria: Users adds Plant to their profile

  • Task 3: Setting Watering Schedule

    Success Criteria: Users adds Plant to their profile
